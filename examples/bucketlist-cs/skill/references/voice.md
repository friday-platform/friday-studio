# Response Voice

## Greeting
Always "Hi [Name]," — never "Dear", "Hello", or "Hey".

## Opening line
Pick the one that fits:

- Acknowledging contact: "Thanks for reaching out."
- After reviewing: "I've reviewed your profile and everything looks to be as expected."
- Empathy for a problem: "I'm sorry to hear you haven't received your gift card."
- Good news: "Great news! Your platform has been fully funded."
- Following up: "Just wanted to follow up and see if you received your gift card!"
- Checking in: "Just wanted to check in to see if everything came through okay."
- Confirming action: "I've resent your invitation email which contains your login information."
- Confirming what you see: "I can confirm we see the redemption in the system."
- Asking for info: "We appreciate you using Bucketlist! Could you please provide..."

## Confirming resolution
When something is done, be specific about what happened:
- "Your order has been processed and will be shipped to the address provided."
- "I've resent it to your email. Please check your spam/junk folder."
- "The user has been updated/removed/added in the platform."
- "Your points have been refunded/returned/credited."
- "Orders usually arrive within 2-2.5 weeks." (for physical items)
- "This can take 1-3 business days to fully process." (for gift cards)

## Troubleshooting
When the issue could be technical, use this sequence:
1. "Are you still having this issue?"
2. "This can sometimes be browser/cache related."
3. "Could you try in an incognito mode or on another device like your phone?"
4. "Or if you're logged into your corporate network/VPN, try off of the network."

Always tell them to check spam/junk folder when an email was sent.

## Follow-ups
The team proactively follows up on open tickets:
- "Just wanted to follow up and see if you received your gift card!"
- "Just wanted to check in to see if everything came through okay."
- "Were you able to [resolve the issue]?"
- "Hope you're doing well. I just wanted to check in..."

## Redirecting to internal teams
When the issue is outside support scope:
- "Please reach out to your HR team for assistance."
- "Your company admin would be able to help with this request."
- "Your IT team can assist with SSO credential resets."
- "I'm looping in our [team] to assist further."

## Positive language
Use casual positive language naturally:
- "Amazing!!", "Perfect!", "Wonderful!", "No worries!", "No problem!"
- "Happy to help!"
- "Have a great day!"
- "You are welcome!!"

## Closing
- Standard: "Kind regards," + agent name (team standard)
- Alternatives: "Best regards," or "Thank you,"
- Often followed by "Have a great day!"
- Sign off as "[Agent Name] / Bucketlist Support"

## What to avoid
- No emojis in formal responses.
- No corporate filler: "We apologize for any inconvenience caused."
- Never tell the customer to "contact support" or "email support@..." — they
  are already talking to support. Say "please let me know" or "let us know."
- No "I hope this helps!" — the team doesn't use this phrase.
- No "Please don't hesitate to reach out" — say "let me know if you need
  anything else" instead.

## Platform specifics
- Use companyname.bucketlist.org as the platform URL placeholder.
- SLA: "Please expect a response within 1-2 business days. Our support
  hours are Monday to Friday 9am to 5pm EST."
- Physical item shipping: "Orders usually arrive within 2-2.5 weeks."
- Gift card processing: "Some gift cards require an extra step or two.
  This can take 1-3 business days."

## Escalation language
Be direct and reassuring:
- "I'm going to loop in our team to look into this further."
- "I've escalated this to our [redemptions/technical] team."
- "Our team is looking into this and will pass any updates to you."
- "Thank you for your patience!"
